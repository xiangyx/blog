## Grammar Summary (Beginner Level)

### 1. Compound Words (Sammensatte ord)
Danish loves to combine words to create new meanings:
- **kæledyr** = kæle (cuddle) + dyr (animal) = pet
- **døgnvagt** = døgn (24 hours) + vagt (guard) = 24-hour service
- **dyrlæge** = dyr (animal) + læge (doctor) = veterinarian
- **medarbejder** = med (with) + arbejder (worker) = employee

**Tip**: When you see a long word, try breaking it into smaller parts!

### 2. Definite Form (Bestemt form)
In Danish, you add endings to make nouns definite (like adding "the" in English):
- **en klinik** → **klinikken** (a clinic → the clinic)
- **et år** → **året** (a year → the year)
- **en måned** → **månedens** (a month → of the month)
- **en læge** → **lægerne** (a doctor → the doctors)

**Pattern for adding "the":**
- Common gender (-en): Add **-en** (klinik → klinikken)
- Neuter gender (-et): Add **-et** (år → året)
- Plural: Add **-ne** or **-ene** (læger → lægerne)

### 3. Passive Voice (Passiv)
Danish uses **-s** ending to make passive verbs (when something is done to the subject):
- **søge** (to seek) → **søges** (is sought/wanted)
- **afholde** (to hold) → **afholdes** (is held)
- **besøge** (to visit) → **besøges** (is visited)

**Example**: Træner søges = Coach is wanted (literally: Coach seeks-itself)

### 4. Modal Verbs (Modalverber)
**skal** (shall/should/must) is commonly used to express obligation or future plans:
- **Så skal du ringe** = Then you should call
- **Skal du købe ny madras?** = Do you need to buy a new mattress?

### 5. Imperatives (Bydeform)
Commands and suggestions use the infinitive form:
- **Meld dig ind!** = Sign up!
- **Få motion!** = Get exercise!
- **Køb online!** = Buy online!

### 6. Prepositions (Præpositioner)
Common prepositions you'll see:
- **hos** = at/with (Hos os = With us)
- **ved** = at/by/in case of (Ved akut sygdom = In case of acute illness)
- **til** = to/for (Til dit kæledyr = For your pet)
- **på** = on/at (På hjemmesiden = On the website)
- **om** = about (Rådgivning om kost = Advice about diet)

### 7. Reflexive Verbs (Refleksive verber)
**sig** is used for reflexive actions (yourself):
- **Meld dig ind** = Sign yourself up (literally: Report yourself in)

### 8. Time Expressions (Tidsudtryk)
- **kl.** = klokken (o'clock)
- **d.** = den (the [date])
- **hver** = every (hver dag = every day)

**Example**: Søndag d. 1. marts kl. 9.00 = Sunday, March 1st at 9 AM

### 9. Abbreviations (Forkortelser)
- **bl.a.** = blandt andet (among other things)
- **kr.** = kroner (Danish currency)
- **d.** = den (the date)
- **kl.** = klokken (o'clock)

### 10. Question Formation (Spørgsmål)
Questions often start with the verb:
- **Har du fået hund?** = Have you gotten a dog?
- **Er du i god form?** = Are you in good shape?
- **Skal du købe madras?** = Do you need to buy a mattress?

**Pattern**: Verb + Subject + Rest of sentence

> Månedens tilbud

> Få motion og en bedre kondi! Meld dig ind i Fitnesshuset nu. Ved indmeldelse i denne måned får du: 1) oprettelse til 0 kr. (normalpris 189,-) 2) 14 dages gratis træning på alle vores hold.

månedens tilbud = offer of the month

better kondi = better fitness

indmeldelse = registration

oprettelse = setup / registration

what is the difference between oprettelse, indmeldelse, meld dig ind and tilmelding?
- "Oprettelse" refers to the process of setting up or creating an account or membership. It focuses on the administrative aspect of registering for something.
- "Indmeldelse" specifically refers to the act of joining or becoming a member of an organization, club, or group. It emphasizes the action of becoming a member.
- "Meld dig ind" is a more informal way of saying "join" or "sign up". It is a direct call to action, encouraging someone to take the step of joining or signing up for something.
- "Tilmelding" refers to the process of registering or signing up for an event, class, or activity. It focuses on the administrative aspect of signing up for something specific.

→ Offer of the month
→ Get exercise and better fitness! Join Fitnesshuset now. By signing up this month, you get: 1) registration for 0 DKK (normal price 189,-) 2) 14 days of free training on all our classes.

what is the difference between rabat and tilbud?
- "Rabat" specifically refers to a reduction in price or a discount offered on a product or service. It focuses on the financial aspect of a price reduction.
- "Tilbud" is a more general term that can refer to any kind of offer, promotion, or deal. It can include discounts (rabat) but also encompasses other types of offers such as special packages, limited-time deals, or exclusive promotions.
