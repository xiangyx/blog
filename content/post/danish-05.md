## Grammar Summary (Beginner Level)

### 1. Perfect Tense with "har" + Past Participle
Danish uses **har** (have) + past participle to express completed actions:
- **Vi har fået** nye varer = We have gotten new goods
- **Vi har ryddet op** = We have cleared up
- **Vi har holdstart** = We have class start

**Common past participles**: fået (gotten), ryddet (cleared), fyldt (filled)

### 2. Compound Words (Sammensatte ord)
Danish creates new words by combining existing ones:
- **kørekort** = køre (drive) + kort (card) = driver's license
- **forårsnyheder** = forår (spring) + nyheder (news) = spring news
- **dametøj** = dame (lady) + tøj (clothing) = women's clothing
- **sovesofa** = sove (sleep) + sofa = sofa bed
- **stueplanter** = stue (living room) + planter (plants) = house plants

**Tip**: Break long words into smaller parts to understand their meaning!

### 3. Passive Voice with "-s" Ending
Use **-s** ending to create passive forms:
- **udlejes** = is rented out (from udleje - to rent out)
- **søges** = is wanted (from søge - to seek)
- **sælger** vs **sælges** = sells vs is sold

**Example**: Fast vikar søges = Permanent temp is wanted

### 4. Reflexive Pronouns
**dig selv** (yourself), **sig selv** (himself/herself/itself/themselves):
- **Forkæl dig selv** = Pamper yourself
- **Vælg selv** = Choose yourself

### 5. Prepositions (Præpositioner)
Common prepositions with specific uses:
- **til** = to/for (Fotos til alt = Photos for everything)
- **med** = with (Med masser af tilbud = With lots of offers)
- **på** = on/at (På lageret = In the storage)
- **mellem** = between (Mellem tre farver = Between three colors)
- **i** = in (I centrum = In the center)

### 6. Present Tense Verbs
Most verbs end in **-er** in present tense:
- **bugner** = is brimming
- **sælger** = sells
- **holder** = keeps/holds
- **klarer** = handles

### 7. Definite Forms ("the")
Add endings to nouns to make them definite:
- **-et**: lager → **lageret** (the storage)
- **-en**: sæson → **sæsonen** (the season)
- **-ne**: varer → **varerne** (the goods)

**Examples**: pollensæsonen (the pollen season), butikken (the store)

### 8. Adjective Agreement
Adjectives must match the gender of nouns:
- Common gender: **en god pris** (a good price)
- Neuter: **et godt tilbud** (a good offer)
- Plural: **gode priser** (good prices)

### 9. Imperatives (Commands)
Use the infinitive form for commands:
- **Vælg** mellem farver = Choose between colors
- **Kom** og hør = Come and hear
- **Kig forbi** = Stop by
- **Ring** og hør = Call and hear

### 10. Common Phrases & Idioms
- **lige nu** = right now
- **bl.a.** = blandt andet = among others
- **komme på tale** = be considered/be a possibility
- **på vej** = on the way
- **kig forbi** = stop by
- **hør nærmere** = hear more details

### 11. Genitive Forms in Compounds
When forming compound nouns, the first noun often takes a genitive form:
- **kvalitets**møbler = quality furniture (kvalitet → kvalitets)
- **forår**snyheder = spring news

### 12. "Der" as Expletive Subject
**Dermed** = thereby/thus (der + med):
- **Dermed** også pollensæsonen = Thus also the pollen season

### 13. Numbers and Measurements
- **stk.** = stykker = pieces
- **kvadratmeter** = square meters
- **kr.** = kroner (Danish currency)

**Example**: 4 stk. = 4 pieces, 95 kvadratmeter = 95 square meters
