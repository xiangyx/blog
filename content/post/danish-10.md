## Grammar Summary (Beginner Level)

### 1. Modal Verb "skal" - Future/Obligation
**Skal** expresses future plans or obligation:
- **Skal du købe** en el-cykel? = Are you going to buy an electric bike?
- Vi **skal** fejre = We are going to celebrate

**Usage**: skal + infinitive

### 2. Compound Words (Sammensatte ord)
Danish creates complex words by combining:
- **el-cykel** = el (electricity) + cykel (bike) = electric bike
- **prøvetur** = prøve (test) + tur (ride) = test ride
- **madlavningshold** = madlavning (cooking) + hold (class) = cooking class
- **jubilæumskoncert** = jubilæum (anniversary) + koncert (concert) = anniversary concert
- **musikbutik** = musik (music) + butik (store) = music store
- **cykelferie** = cykel (bike) + ferie (holiday) = bike holiday
- **festlokaler** = fest (party) + lokaler (premises) = party venues
- **musikanlæg** = musik (music) + anlæg (system) = music system
- **diskokugle** = disko (disco) + kugle (ball) = disco ball

### 3. "Står klar" - Idiomatic Expression
Meaning "are ready" or "stand ready":
- **Vi står klar** med råd = We are ready with advice
- **står klar** = stand ready

### 4. Superlative Forms
**Bedst** and **-ste** ending for superlatives:
- god → bedre → **bedst** = good → better → best
- **passer bedst** = fits best
- hyggelig → **hyggeligste** = cozy → coziest

### 5. "Både ... og" - Both ... and
Correlative conjunction:
- **Både** nye **og** brugte = Both new and used
- **Både** børn **og** voksne = Both children and adults

### 6. "Byder på" - Offers
Idiomatic expression meaning "offers":
- **Byder på** mange nye hold = Offers many new classes
- **Vi byder på** sandwich = We offer sandwiches

### 7. Imperative + Preposition Movement
Commands with prepositions:
- **Kig forbi** = Drop by (literally: look past)
- **Tag med** = Take along
- **Læs mere** = Read more
- **Book jeres ferie** = Book your vacation

### 8. "Drømmer om" - Dreams of
Construction with "om":
- **Drømmer dit barn om** at lære = Does your child dream of learning
- **drømme om** + infinitive/noun

### 9. Definite Forms ("the")
Add endings to make nouns definite:
- **-en**: skov → **skoven** (the forest)
- **-et**: barn → **barnet** (the child) 
- **-ne**: øens → **øens** (the island's - genitive)

### 10. Genitive Form with "-s"
Show possession:
- **øens** hyggeligste hoteller = the island's coziest hotels
- **30-års** jubilæum = 30-year anniversary
- **jeres** ferie = your (plural) vacation

### 11. Present Tense Verbs
Most verbs end in **-r** or **-er**:
- **finder** = find (from finde)
- **passer** = fits (from passe)
- **byder** = offers (from byde)
- **fejrer** = celebrate (from fejre)
- **koster** = costs (from koste)
- **cykler** = cycle (from cykle)
- **underviser** = teach (from undervise)

### 12. Passive Voice with "-s"
**-s** ending creates passive:
- **udlejes** = for rent/is rented out (from udleje)

### 13. Adjective Agreement
Adjectives change based on definiteness and number:
- Indefinite common: **en gratis prøvetur** = a free test ride
- Indefinite neuter: **et stort udvalg** = a large selection
- Plural: **spiselige planter** = edible plants
- Definite superlative: **hyggeligste hoteller** = coziest hotels

### 14. "Let at" Construction - Easy to
Pattern: adjective + at + infinitive:
- **Let at lave** = Easy to make
- **Let at finde** = Easy to find

### 15. Time Expressions
Common Danish time phrases:
- **om dagen** = per day
- **i alderen 5-18** = aged 5-18
- **ca. to timer** = about two hours
- **op til 50 personer** = up to 50 people

### 16. Prepositions in Context
Common prepositions:
- **til** = to/for (Råd til tilberedning = Advice for preparation)
- **på** = on (På Bornholm = On Bornholm)
- **i** = in (I skoven = In the forest)
- **med** = with (Tag med = Take along)
- **mellem** = between (Mellem hoteller = Between hotels)
- **om** = about (Drømmer om = Dream about)
- **fra** = from (Priser fra 2.495 kr = Prices from 2,495 kr)

### 17. "Mulighed for" - Possibility of
Common construction:
- **Mulighed for tilkøb** = Possibility of additional purchase
- **mulighed for** + noun/infinitive

### 18. "Op til" - Up to
Expression meaning "up to":
- **Op til 50 personer** = Up to 50 people
- **op til** + number

### 19. Plural Formation
Regular plural patterns:
- Add **-e**: plante → **planter** = plants
- Add **-er**: billet → **billetter** = tickets, hotel → **hoteller** = hotels
- Add **-r**: cykel → **cykler** = bicycles
- Irregular: barn → **børn** = children

### 20. "Bliv ven med" - Become Friends with
Idiomatic expression:
- **Bliv ven med** din wok = Become friends with your wok
- **bliv ven med** + object

### 21. "Yderligere" - Further
Formal word for "additional" or "further":
- **Yderligere information** = Further information
- More formal than "mere" (more)

### 22. Abbreviations
Common Danish abbreviations:
- **fx** = for eksempel = for example
- **kr.** = kroner = Danish currency
- **ca.** = cirka = approximately
- **d.** = den = the (for dates)
- **inkl.** = inklusiv = including

### 23. Geographic Prepositions
Different prepositions for places:
- **på** Bornholm = on Bornholm (island)
- **i** Aarhus = in Aarhus (city)

### 24. "Centralt beliggende" - Centrally Located
Common real estate phrase:
- **Centralt beliggende** = Centrally located
- Pattern: adverb + past participle
