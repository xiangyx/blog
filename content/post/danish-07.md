## Grammar Summary (Beginner Level)

### 1. Passive Voice with "-s" Ending
Common pattern for "is/are + past participle":
- **søges** = is sought/wanted (søge → søges)
- **udlejes** = for rent/is rented out (udleje → udlejes)
- **fører** vs **føres** = carry vs is carried
- **tilbyder** vs **tilbydes** = offer vs is offered

**Examples**: 
- Medlemmer søges = Members are wanted
- Lejlighed udlejes = Apartment for rent

### 2. Compound Words (Sammensatte ord)
Danish combines multiple words to create new meanings:
- **vaskemaskine** = vaske (wash) + maskine (machine) = washing machine
- **aftensmad** = aften (evening) + mad (food) = dinner
- **kogebog** = koge (cook) + bog (book) = cookbook
- **butikslokale** = butik (store) + lokale (premises) = store premises
- **vaskekælder** = vaske (wash) + kælder (basement) = laundry basement
- **loftsrum** = loft (attic) + rum (room) = attic space
- **weekendophold** = weekend + ophold (stay) = weekend stay
- **løbetøj** = løbe (run) + tøj (clothes) = running clothes
- **ikke-ryger** = ikke (not) + ryger (smoker) = non-smoker

**Tip**: Break down compound words into parts!

### 3. Possessive Forms
Danish possessives agree with the noun's gender:
- Common gender: **egen** parkeringsplads = own parking space
- Neuter gender: **eget** loftsrum = own attic space
- Plural: **egne** børn = own children

### 4. "Gå i stykker" - Idiomatic Expression
Literally "go in pieces" = to break down:
- **Er maskinen gået i stykker?** = Has the machine broken down?
- **Bilen går i stykker** = The car breaks down

### 5. Present Tense Verbs
Most verbs end in **-r** or **-er**:
- **søger** = is looking for
- **sender** = send
- **fører** = carry/lead
- **håber** = hope
- **tilbyder** = offer

### 6. Present Perfect with "har" + Past Participle
Express completed actions:
- **har gået i stykker** = has broken down
- **har interesse** = has interest

### 7. Relative Clauses with "som"
"som" means "that/which/who" in relative clauses:
- Retter **som** børn kan lide = Dishes **that** children like
- En person **som** jeg kender = A person **that** I know

### 8. Prepositions
Common prepositions with specific uses:
- **til** = to/for (Alt til din træning = Everything for your training)
- **på** = on/at (På nettet = On the internet)
- **i** = in (I centrum = In the center)
- **inden for** = within (Inden for 24 timer = Within 24 hours)
- **af** = of (En af vores teknikere = One of our technicians)

### 9. "Uanset" - Regardless of
Used to express "no matter" or "regardless of":
- **Uanset niveau** = Regardless of level
- **Uanset alder** = Regardless of age

### 10. Definite Forms ("the")
Add endings to make nouns definite:
- **-en**: maskine → **maskinen** (the machine)
- **-et**: nettet → **nettet** (the internet)
- **-ne**: medlemmer → **medlemmerne** (the members)

### 11. "Hører med" - Is Included
Idiomatic expression meaning "belongs with" or "is included":
- **Parkering hører med** = Parking is included
- **Det hører med** = It is included

**Literal**: høre (hear/belong) + med (with)

### 12. Negation with "ikke"
**ikke** is placed after the verb:
- Husdyr **ikke tilladt** = Pets not allowed
- Jeg er **ikke-ryger** = I am a non-smoker (as compound)

### 13. Time Expressions
- **inden da** = before then
- **pr. måned** = per month
- **i denne uge** = this week
- **den 14. januar** = on January 14th
- **inden for 24 timer** = within 24 hours

### 14. Abbreviations
Common Danish abbreviations:
- **inkl.** = inklusiv = including
- **pr.** = per = per
- **m2** = kvadratmeter = square meters
- **kr.** = kroner = Danish currency

### 15. Imperatives (Commands)
Use infinitive form directly:
- **Ring** = Call
- **Book** = Book
- **Se** = See
- **Køb** = Buy

### 16. "Alt" Constructions
**Alt** can mean "everything" or "all":
- **Alt til din træning** = Everything for your training
- **Alt har interesse** = Everything is of interest (idiomatic)

### 17. "Både ... og" - Both ... and
Used to connect two items:
- **Både** stationære **og** bærbare = Both desktop and laptop
