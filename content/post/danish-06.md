## Grammar Summary (Beginner Level)

### 1. Question Formation with "har du brug for"
Common phrase for asking "do you need":
- **Har du brug for** et fritidsjob? = Do you need a part-time job?
- **Har du brug for** hjælp? = Do you need help?

**Literal**: har (have) + du (you) + brug (need) + for (for) = have you need for

### 2. Compound Words (Sammensatte ord)
Danish creates many compound nouns:
- **fritidsjob** = fritid (free time) + job = part-time job
- **autoværksted** = auto (car) + værksted (workshop) = car workshop
- **serviceeftersyn** = service + efter (after) + syn (inspection) = service inspection
- **babyudstyr** = baby + udstyr (equipment) = baby equipment
- **pusleborde** = pusle (change diaper) + borde (tables) = changing tables
- **tremmesenge** = tremme (bar) + senge (beds) = cribs
- **bæreseler** = bære (carry) + seler (straps) = baby carriers
- **svømmehal** = svømme (swim) + hal (hall) = swimming pool
- **børnepasning** = børne (children) + pasning (care) = babysitting
- **hundeluftning** = hunde (dogs) + luftning (airing) = dog walking

**Tip**: Break down long words into parts to understand their meaning!

### 3. Passive Voice with "-s" or "bliver/er + past participle"
Actions where the subject receives the action:
- **søges** = is wanted (from søge - to seek)
- **sælges** = for sale (from sælge - to sell)
- **udføres** = is performed (from udføre - to perform)
- **leveres** = is delivered (from levere - to deliver)
- **foregår** = takes place (from foregå - to take place)

**Example**: Reparationer udføres = Repairs are performed

### 4. Reflexive Pronouns
**sig** (oneself), **dig selv** (yourself):
- **Beskytte sig** mod solen = Protect oneself from the sun
- **Forkæl dig selv** = Pamper yourself

### 5. Possessive Forms
Possessives agree with the noun:
- **egen bil** (common gender) = own car
- **eget hus** (neuter) = own house
- **egne børn** (plural) = own children
- **barnets første år** = the child's first year (genitive-s)

### 6. Adjective Endings
Adjectives change based on gender and definiteness:
- Indefinite common: **en erfaren** mekaniker = an experienced mechanic
- Indefinite neuter: **et dejligt** ophold = a lovely stay
- Definite/plural: **de erfarne** mekanikere = the experienced mechanics
- Plural: **populære mærker** = popular brands

### 7. Present Tense Verbs
Most verbs end in **-r** or **-er**:
- **savner** = miss
- **foregår** = takes place
- **udføres** = is performed
- **leveres** = is delivered

### 8. Past Participles
Used with **har** to form perfect tense:
- **har kørt** = has driven
- **nyåbnet** = newly opened (ny + åbnet)
- **nysynet** = newly inspected (ny + synet)

### 9. Prepositions for Various Uses
Common prepositions:
- **til** = to/for (Alt til din baby = Everything for your baby)
- **i** = in (I din fritid = In your free time)
- **på** = on/at (På et hotel = At a hotel)
- **mod** = against (Mod solen = Against the sun)
- **med** = with (Med nye bremser = With new brakes)
- **af** = of (Udlejning af biler = Rental of cars)

### 10. Imperatives (Commands)
Use the infinitive form directly:
- **Bliv** omdeler = Become a distributor
- **Book** en tid = Book an appointment
- **Læs** mere = Read more
- **Giv** din baby = Give your baby

### 11. Common Abbreviations
- **ca.** = cirka = approximately
- **fx** = for eksempel = for example
- **kr.** = kroner = Danish currency

### 12. "Der" Constructions
**Der** can introduce existence:
- **hvor der er** garanti = where there is guarantee

### 13. Time Expressions
- **i din fritid** = in your free time
- **primært i weekenden** = primarily on weekends
- **ca. 10 timer** = approximately 10 hours
- **pr. dag** = per day
- **efter skole** = after school
- **alle ugens hverdage** = all weekdays of the week

### 14. Definite Forms ("the")
Add endings to make nouns definite:
- **-en**: forælder → **forældrene** (the parents)
- **-et**: værksted → **værkstedet** (the workshop)
- **-ne**: reparationer → **reparationerne** (the repairs)

### 15. Double Compound Adjectives
Using **ny-** (new) prefix:
- **nyåbnet** = newly opened
- **nysynet** = newly inspected
- **nyfødte** = newborn
