# Summary

## Most frequent (short) words & useful extra meanings

> Notes: Frequency is approximate (from this document). Meanings include common alternatives. Examples are simple and beginner-friendly.

### **jeg** (≈ many)
- **Meaning:** I
- **Also seen as:** *mig* (me), *min/mine* (my)
- **Examples:**
  - **Jeg** synger på dansk. → I sing in Danish.
  - Folk kommer hen til **mig**. → People come up to me.

### **det** (≈ many)
- **Meanings:** it / that; sometimes “dummy” subject (“it is…”) / general “this situation”
- **Examples:**
  - **Det** går ok. → It’s going okay.
  - **Det** er hårdt. → It’s tough.

### **og** (≈ many)
- **Meaning:** and (links words/clauses)
- **Example:** Jeg synger, **og** jeg skriver sange. → I sing, and I write songs.

### **at** (≈ many)
- **Meanings:** to (infinitive marker); “that” in some fixed patterns (not used as “that” here)
- **Examples:**
  - at synge → to sing
  - at spille → to play (an instrument)

### **er** (≈ many)
- **Meaning:** is/are (present of *at være*)
- **Example:** Det **er** mange overraskede over. → Many are surprised by that.

### **har** (≈ many)
- **Meanings:** have; auxiliary for perfect tense (“have + past participle”)
- **Examples:**
  - Jeg **har** en bred musiksmag. → I have a broad taste in music.
  - Jeg **har** været glad for at synge. → I have been happy to sing.

### **på** (≈ often)
- **Meanings:** on; in/at (language/medium); about (depending on phrase)
- **Examples:**
  - på en scene → on a stage
  - på dansk / på engelsk → in Danish / in English

### **i** (≈ often)
- **Meanings:** in (time/place); during
- **Examples:**
  - i fremtiden → in the future
  - i udlandet → abroad (lit. “in the abroad”)

### **til** (≈ often)
- **Meanings:** to; for; at (destination/purpose)
- **Examples:**
  - gå til undervisning → go to lessons
  - nødt til at… → have to…

### **med** (≈ often)
- **Meanings:** with; by/using
- **Examples:**
  - blander pop **med** lidt jazz → mix pop with a bit of jazz
  - succes **med** min musik → success with my music

### **så** (≈ often)
- **Meanings:** so/therefore; then (sequence)
- **Examples:**
  - Jeg var 10, **så** ville jeg lære et instrument. → I was 10, so I wanted to learn an instrument.

### **når** (≈ a few)
- **Meanings:** when (often for repeated/general situations); “when/if” in habits
- **Examples:**
  - **Når** jeg gør det, kan jeg mærke det. → When I do that, I can feel it.

### **fordi** (≈ a few)
- **Meaning:** because
- **Example:** Jeg hører heavy rock, **fordi** der er god energi i den musik. → I listen to heavy rock because there’s good energy in that music.

### **selv** / **selvfølgelig** (≈ a few)
- **Meanings:** self; of course
- **Examples:**
  - jeg skriver **selv** mine sange → I write my own songs myself
  - **Selvfølgelig** er det forskelligt… → Of course it varies…

### **jo** (≈ a few)
- **Meaning:** discourse particle: “as you know / after all / you see” (adds a shared/obvious tone)
- **Example:** Nu skriver jeg **jo** selv mine sange. → Now I do write my own songs (as you know/after all).

### **ikke** (≈ a few)
- **Meaning:** not
- **Example:** Det synes jeg **ikke**. → I don’t think so.

## Beginner grammar takeaways (very concise)

1. **Main-clause word order (V2):** the verb is usually 2nd.
   - *Lige nu **går** det fint.* / *Selvfølgelig **er** det forskelligt…*

2. **Infinitive with _at_ (“to”):**
   - *at synge, at spille, at lære*.

3. **Perfect tense:** **har** + past participle.
   - *Jeg **har været** glad…* / *…oplevelser jeg selv **har haft**.*

4. **Modal verbs:** **kan** (can), **vil** (will), **skal** (shall/need to), **ville** (wanted/would).
   - *jeg **kan** leve af…* / *jeg **vil** gerne…* / *jeg **skal** have succes…*

5. **Fixed necessity phrase:** **bliver nødt til at** = will have to.
   - *jeg **bliver nødt til at** skrive på engelsk.*

6. **Subordinate clauses:** words like **fordi, når, at, hvor** introduce clauses; the verb tends to come later.
   - *…fordi jeg synes, der **er** en god energi…*

7. **Common preposition phrases:**
   - *gå **til** undervisning* (go to lessons), *leve **af** musik* (live off), *tænke **over** noget* (think about).
