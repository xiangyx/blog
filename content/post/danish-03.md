## Grammatical notes

- **Imperative (commands)**: verb comes first; used a lot in ads/instructions. Examples: *Book en gratis prøvetime*, *Besøg vores familievenlige restaurant*, *Tilmeld dig …*, *Kom til gratis foredrag*.
- **Reflexive pronoun** *dig* (“yourself”): common after commands like *Tilmeld dig*.
- **Possessives (agreement)**: *din* (common gender), *dit* (neuter), *dine* (plural), plus *vores*. Examples: *din indbakke*, *dit barns skolestart*, *dine venner*, *vores hjemmeside*.
- **Noun gender + articles**: *en/et* matter for adjectives and possessives. Examples in the text: *en gratis prøvetime*, *et stort udvalg*.
- **Definite forms (“the”)**: suffixes on the noun, often *-en/-et*; sometimes *-n* after words ending in *-e*. Examples: *shoppingturen* (“the shopping trip”), *indbakken* (pattern), *Dyrehandlen*.
- **Adjective agreement**: adjective matches gender/number. Examples: *en gratis prøvetime* vs *et stort udvalg*; plural: *glade og sunde kæledyr*.
- **Present tense (nutid)**: many verbs end in *-er*. Examples: *tilbyder*, *træner*, *sælger*, *søger*, *hjælper*.
- **Modal verbs + infinitive**: very common for ability/intent/necessity. Examples: *Kan du hjælpe …?*, *Jeg vil gerne træne …*, *Jeg skal have styr på …*.
- **Infinitive with** *at*: appears in fixed phrases and after verbs like *lære*. Examples: *har lyst til at læse med*, *lære at lave*.
- **Passive -s**: action without naming who does it. Example: *Foredraget afholdes torsdag* (“The talk is held Thursday”).
- **Relative clauses with** *som*: “who/that/which”. Example: *frivillige, som har lyst til at læse med …*.
- **Word order (V2) + inversion**: verb is typically 2nd in main clauses; if you start with time/place, the subject moves after the verb. Example: *I hele denne uge har vi rabat …*.
- **Core prepositions** (easy to mix up): *i* (in), *på* (on/at), *til* (to/for), *med* (with), *inden for* (within a field). Examples: *i naturen*, *på vores hjemmeside*, *til glade kunder*, *hold med instruktør*, *inden for marketing*.
