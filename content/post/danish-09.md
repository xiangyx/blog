## Grammar Summary (Beginner Level)

### 1. Reflexive Verbs with "sig"
Many verbs use reflexive pronouns:
- **gifte sig** = to marry (literally: marry oneself)
- **skynde sig** = to hurry (literally: hurry oneself)

**Examples**:
- Vi skal giftes = We will get married
- Skynd dig! = Hurry up!

### 2. "Går på pension" - Retirement Expression
Idiomatic phrase meaning "to retire":
- **går på pension** = are retiring
- **gå på pension** = to retire

**Structure**: gå (go) + på (on) + pension (retirement)

### 3. Compound Words (Sammensatte ord)
Danish creates complex words by combining:
- **afskedsreception** = afsked (farewell) + reception = farewell reception
- **samarbejdspartnere** = samarbejds (collaboration) + partnere (partners) = collaborators
- **designermøbler** = designer + møbler (furniture) = designer furniture
- **åbningstilbud** = åbning (opening) + tilbud (offer) = opening offer
- **slotspark** = slot (castle) + park = castle park
- **festsal** = fest (party) + sal (hall) = banquet hall
- **forlovelsesringe** = forlovelse (engagement) + ringe (rings) = engagement rings
- **vielsesringe** = vielse (wedding) + ringe (rings) = wedding rings
- **laserbehandling** = laser + behandling (treatment) = laser treatment
- **specialdesignede** = special + designede (designed) = custom-designed

### 4. Genitive "-s" in Compounds
Use **-s** to connect nouns:
- **samarbejdspartnere** = collaboration + s + partners
- **mange års erfaring** = many years' experience

### 5. Definite Forms ("the")
Add endings to make nouns definite:
- **-en**: operation → **operationen** (the operation)
- **-et**: overskud → **overskuddet** (the profit)
- **-ne**: partnere → **partnerne** (the partners)

### 6. Present Tense Verbs
Most verbs end in **-r** or **-er**:
- **åbner** = opens (from åbne)
- **inviterer** = invite (from invitere)
- **byder** = offers (from byde)
- **spiller** = plays (from spille)

### 7. Superlative Forms
**Bedste** is the superlative of "god":
- god → bedre → **bedst** = good → better → best
- **byens bedste** = the city's best

### 8. "Træt af" - Tired of
Construction meaning "tired of":
- **Træt af** at bruge briller = Tired of wearing glasses
- Pattern: træt + af + infinitive/noun

### 9. Adjective Agreement
Adjectives change based on definiteness and number:
- Indefinite common: **en moderne behandling** = a modern treatment
- Neuter: **et godt tilbud** = a good offer
- Plural: **fine gamle sofaer** = fine old sofas
- Definite: **det sidste nye** = the latest new

### 10. "Op til" - Up to
Expression meaning "up to":
- **Op til** 40 personer = Up to 40 people
- **spiller op** til dans = play music for dancing

### 11. Prepositions in Context
Common prepositions:
- **på** = on (Går på pension = Retire; Halv pris på = Half price on)
- **til** = to/for (Musik til din fest = Music for your party)
- **i** = in (I denne måned = This month)
- **af** = of (Træt af = Tired of)
- **hos** = at (Hos os = At our place)
- **inden for** = within (Inden for design = Within design)

### 12. Passive Voice with "-s"
Common passive constructions:
- **giftes** = get married (from gifte sig)
- But note: Most are reflexive, not truly passive

### 13. Time Expressions
Common Danish time phrases:
- **lige nu** = right now
- **denne dag** = this day
- **første uge** = first week
- **mange år** = many years
- **op til** = up to

### 14. "Kig" Expressions
**Kig** (look) creates several phrases:
- **Kig ind** = drop by, look in
- **Kig forbi** = drop by
- **at kigge** = to look

### 15. "Byde på" - To Offer
Common expression for offering:
- **Vi byder på** sandwich = We offer sandwiches
- **byde** can also mean "to invite"

### 16. Plural Formation
Regular plural patterns:
- Add **-r**: ring → **ringe** = rings
- Add **-er**: partner → **partnere** = partners
- Add **-e**: sofa → **sofaer** = sofas  
- Irregular: møbel → **møbler** = furniture

### 17. "Hele" - Whole/All
**Hele** means "whole" or "all":
- **hele verden** = the whole world
- **det hele** = all of it
- Always with definite form

### 18. Imperatives (Commands)
Use infinitive form:
- **Skynd dig!** = Hurry up!
- **Kom** = Come
- **Se** = See/Look

### 19. "Kun" and "Bare" - Only/Just
Used for limitation or encouragement:
- **kun** 3500 kr = only 3500 kr
- **Bare** kig forbi = Just drop by

### 20. Geographic Names
Danish regions:
- **Fyn** = Funen (island)
- **Jylland** = Jutland (peninsula)
- Use **i** (in) with Jylland, **på** (on) with Fyn

### 21. "Inkl." - Including
Common abbreviation:
- **inkl.** = inklusiv = including
- Pris inkl. transport = Price including transportation

> Afskedsreception i Brillebixen
>
> Vi går på pension og vil gerne sige pænt farvel til vores gode kunder og samarbejdspartnere. Derfor inviterer vi til reception: d. 20. februar kl. 15-18 i Brillebixen. På denne dag byder vi på gratis sandwich og et glas vin.

Afskedsreception = Farewell reception
 - decompound: afsked + s + reception
 - afsked = farewell
 - words having prefix "af-" often indicate a separation or removal, for example:
   - afgå (to depart)
   - aflyse (to cancel), lyse means "to light"

pænt = nicely, politely

samarbejdspartnere = collaborators, partners

byder = offers, invites
 - verb: at byde (to offer, to invite)
 - verb ends with "r" in present tense for "vi" (we)

→ Farewell reception at Brillebixen

We are retiring and would like to say a proper goodbye to our good customers and collaborators. Therefore, we invite you to a reception: on February 20th from 3 PM to 6 PM at Brillebixen. On this day, we offer free sandwiches and a glass of wine.
